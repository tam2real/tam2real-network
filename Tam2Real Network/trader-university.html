01
Foundation
SAVE FIRST. ALWAYS.
Before you invest, trade, or spend — pay yourself first. Set aside a percentage of every dollar that comes in before anything else. Most people save what's left over. That's why most people stay broke. Flip the order.
Rule: Save at least 20% of every dollar you earn. Non-negotiable.
02
The Mindset Shift
INVEST MORE THAN YOU SAVE
Saving money in a bank account loses value every year due to inflation — around 3–4% per year the dollar gets weaker. Your savings sitting still is actually going backwards. The goal is to flip the ratio: invest more than you save. Put money into assets that grow — index funds, ETFs, real estate, businesses — so your money is working while you sleep.
Cash in a savings account = slowly losing. Money in assets = compounding and growing.
03
Compound Interest
MONEY MAKING MONEY
Compound interest is the 8th wonder of the world. When your money earns returns, those returns also earn returns. $1,000 invested at 10% annually becomes $1,100 after year one. But year two it's not $1,100 + $100 — it's $1,210 because the $100 also earned 10%. The longer you leave it, the faster it snowballs. Time in the market beats timing the market.
Start early. Even $50/month invested at 20 years old beats $500/month at 40.
04
The 4% Rule · Financial Freedom
25X YOUR EXPENSES = FREEDOM NUMBER
The 4% Rule is the foundation of financial independence. Take your annual expenses and multiply by 25 — that's your "freedom number." Once your invested portfolio hits that number, you can withdraw 4% per year forever without running out of money (historically). Your investments grow faster than you withdraw.
Example: Spend $40,000/year? Freedom number = $1,000,000.
At $1M invested, withdraw $40,000/year (4%) and your money keeps growing.
05
The Order of Operations
HOW TO STACK THE RIGHT WAY
Most people have no system. Here's the system — in order of priority:
1 Build a 3–6 month emergency fund first. No exceptions.
2 Pay off high-interest debt (credit cards, payday loans).
3 Max out tax-advantaged accounts (Roth IRA, 401k if available).
4 Invest the rest in index funds, ETFs, or assets you understand.
5 Only then — speculate, trade, and take calculated risks.
